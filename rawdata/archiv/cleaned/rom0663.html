150 Years of United Italy - The American University of Rome, 8 April 2011 Co-sponsored by: The Association for the Study of Modern Italy (ASMI) The British School at Rome The American Academy of Rome The Norwegian Academy of Rome The American University of Rome's Department of International Relations will be holding a one-day international conference on 8 April 2011 to mark the 150th anniversary of the unification of Italy. The conference will focus on the effects of unification on a broad range of topics and fields, it will analyze a wide range of issues connected to the successes and failures of nation-building in Italy over the past century and a half. Papers are welcome across the subject disciplines of the arts, humanities and social sciences as which directly engage with the consequences of unification and the complete or incomplete processes of nationbuilding. We particularly invite contributions that relate to one or more of the following themes: Political, economic and social consequences, past and present Images of the nation through literature and media Personality cults Artistic, architectural and archaeological expressions of and challenges to the nation Italy as a (post)colonial nation Italyâ€™s international relations The Southern Question Migration Borders and boundaries Religion The conference will be held in English. Proposals (c. 300 words) are invited from both established and unpublished scholars in the relevant disciplines.

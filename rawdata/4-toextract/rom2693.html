Professor of Romance Linguistics (1,0 fte) University of Groningen Vacancy number 210312 Job description The Faculty of Arts of the University of Groningen is seeking to appoint a Full Professor of Romance Linguistics (1,0 fte). The Professor will be responsible for teaching and research in this field. The successful candidate will have proven interest and competence in Romance linguistics, understood broadly (i.e., including, but not restricted to comparative Romance linguistics), and may be a specialist in French, Italian and/or Spanish linguistics. The professor must be an internationally recognized expert in at least one subfield of Romance linguistics and/or theoretical approaches of linguistics. The position involves: - co-ordinating and teaching courses in the above-mentioned field in the Bachelor's and Master's degree programmes in Romance languages and cultures and the research Master Linguistics - co-ordinating and conducting research in Romance linguistics - raising external funds for research - supervising PhD students - performing administrative tasks within and outside the university - further cultivating and developing national and international contacts to benefit the chair's teaching and research Requirements We are seeking candidates who want to lead a small, enthusiastic team of linguists in the department of Romance languages and cultures. We assume that interested parties: - have a PhD in the field of Romance linguistics - are experienced lecturers and active researchers, especially in the field of Romance linguistics - have good administrative and organizational skills - have been successful at raising external funds for research - have experience in supervising PhD students - are able to promote interest in the field - are prepared to move to the Groningen area. - have a good command of the Dutch language or are prepared to learn Dutch within a short term. Conditions of employment The University of Groningen offers an appointment as Full Professor 2 as of 1 September 2011 or sooner if possible. The salary will be € 4,904 (minimum)/ € 7,142 (maximum) gross per month for full-time employment (CLA for Dutch universities). If you would like to draw our attention to suitably qualified candidates, please write to the director of the Department of Personnel and Organization within two weeks. Organisation Since its foundation in 1614, the University of Groningen has enjoyed an international reputation as a dynamic and innovative centre of higher education offering high-quality teaching and research. Balanced study and career paths in a wide variety of disciplines encourage the 27,000 students and researchers to develop their own individual talents. Belonging to the best research universities in Europe and joining forces with prestigious partner universities and networks, the University of Groningen is truly an international place of knowledge. The Faculty of Arts is a large, dynamic faculty in the heart of the city of Groningen. It has more than 5000 students and 700 staff members, who are working at the frontiers of knowledge every day. The Faculty offers a wide range of degree programmes: 19 Bachelor's programmes and over 35 Master's programmes. Our research, which is internationally widely acclaimed, covers the following fields: Archaeology, Cultural Studies, History, International Relations, Journalism, Language and Literary Studies, and Linguistics.

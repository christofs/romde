Lector in French Manchester Metropolitan University -Department of Languages, Information and Communications (Full Time) / (Fixed-term until 31/07/2014) Grade 6: £23, 811 - £26, 779 per annum pro-rata We are looking to appoint a dynamic and committed full time French Language Lector. The successful candidate will be based in the Department of Languages, Information and Communications and will mainly be working for the BA(Hons) Language Programme and Uniwide, the Institution-wide language scheme intended primarily for students who are not taking specialist language degrees. Applications are invited from candidates with native or near-native language proficiency with a higher degree/postgraduate qualification in teaching French as a foreign language or equivalent. You will be able to demonstrate significant experience of teaching and administration in a higher-education setting, with the ability to teach across all levels of the subject. You will also demonstrate evidence of teamwork and initiative in the context of a language teaching programme and of critical reflection on the process of teaching and of the development of teaching and study materials. Candidates must demonstrate an appreciation of modern communicative approaches to language teaching and a willingness to develop resources to support teaching and learning. Knowledge of technology-enhanced learning would be an advantage, as would recent experience of teaching French language in Higher Education in the UK. For an informal discussion regarding the requirements of the role, please contact Dr Cemi Belkacemi (c.belkacemi@mmu.ac.uk). For more information and to apply online, visit www.mmu.ac.uk/jobs. If you do not have access to the internet or require an application in an alternative format please contact Human Resources on 0161 247 3502 quoting reference G2/2174. Closing date for receipt of applications is 17/09/2012. Applications must be submitted by midnight on the closing date. MMU values diversity and welcomes applications from all sections of the community.

Cemi Belkacemi, 03.09.2012

Lectureship in French Bangor University - College of Arts, Education and Humanities School of Modern Languages Grade 7: £29,972 - £35,788 p.a Applications are invited from candidates for a Lectureship in French. The successful candidate will have acquired, or be close to acquiring, a doctorate in a relevant field of study. He or she should have a high degree of competence in spoken and written French. (Native speakers of French should be thoroughly competent in English). The successful candidate will be involved in teaching language and culture at all levels, including a contribution to the School's MA provision. This vacancy represents an opportunity for an ambitious academic to work in a dynamic, supportive and friendly School. The successful applicant will have a research focus on modern French culture and society (post-1789), and preference may be given to candidates whose research specialism encompasses French literature, history, film or music of the 19th, 20th or 21st centuries. The post is available from 14 January 2012. Application forms and further particulars should be obtained by contacting Human Resources, Bangor University, tel: (01248) 382926/383865, e-mail: personnel@bangor.ac.uk, web: www.bangor.ac.uk Please quote reference number 11-11/42 when applying. Closing date for applications: 1.00 p.m. Friday 7th October 2011. For any informal enquiries about the post, please contact the Head of School, Dr Anna Saunders (tel. 01248 382135, email: a.saunders@bangor.ac.uk). Committed To Equal Opportunities
